Spotlight: Total Crashes by Track Type

This case study explores crash dynamics on simulated racing tracks with a focused lens on Urban environments. Using R and ggplot2, the analysis combines player behavior, car performance, and track design to reveal patterns in crash frequency and race outcomes.

Dataset: 10 players × 100 races — variables include lap time, speed, car type, and track type.

Key Findings

  • Urban tracks have the highest crash rate yet the best average race position.
  • Player P010 crashes most often, while P003 consistently ranks highest.
  • Japanese car types show elevated crash counts on Urban tracks.
  • Speed and lap time affect crash frequency, though not always predictably.
  • Multi-variable diagnostics uncover risky player–car–track combinations.

Insight

Urban tracks record the highest crash count, followed by Desert and Circuit. The likely reason: tighter turns, unpredictable layouts, and complex visuals.

Conclusion: Urban environments present the greatest safety challenge — setting the foundation for deeper player and car performance diagnostics.

Chart 1: Total Crashes by Track Type — Urban leads in crash frequency.

Lap Time vs. Crashes on Urban Tracks (Trend Line)

Each point is a lap, colored by crash intensity. The dashed trend line shows a slight upward slope, suggesting a modest correlation between longer lap times and higher crash frequency.

Conclusion: Longer laps may signal poor control, fatigue, or inefficient strategy. This trend supports deeper investigation into pacing, player skill, and track complexity.

Player Performance Notes

  • P003 — Best average position; stable across tracks.
  • P010 — Highest Urban crash intensity; priority for coaching.
  • P007 — Generally lower crash footprint.

Urban Track Crashes by Car Type

Crash totals on Urban tracks by car origin: Japan (JPN), USA, Canada (CAN), and Brazil (BRA).

JPN records the highest (~25), followed by USA (~17), CAN (~10), and BRA (~5).

Conclusion

Car type shapes Urban crash frequency. JPN models may be faster or more sensitive, requiring handling/tuning changes and targeted driver training for dense layouts.

Average Race Position by Player

Rankings by average race position across all tracks: P003 leads (~2) while P008 trails (~6.5).

Conclusion

P003 shows consistent control and strategy; P008 may benefit from support in car choice, track familiarity, and driving tactics — insights useful for coaching and matchmaking.

Urban Track Crash Diagnostic

Lap time vs. crash count; color = player ID, shape = car type, size = speed. Clusters of higher crashes appear around the 90–110s lap-time window for certain players/car types.

  • Crash risk reflects interplay of lap time, speed, player behavior, and car dynamics.
  • Specific player–car combinations at higher speeds drive more crashes within the critical window.
  • Use to target tuning, driver coaching, and track redesign.

Key Takeaways

  • Urban has the highest crash rate.
  • P010 concentrates risk; P003 remains consistent.
  • JPN cars need handling adjustments on Urban layouts.

Recommendations

  • Coaching for high-risk players with Urban-specific drills.
  • Car tuning for Urban (traction/handling) — especially for JPN type.
  • Use diagnostics to inform track redesign or adaptive difficulty.
